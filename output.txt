[[File:Helix_Piercing-1.jpg|thumb|right|Helix Piercing]]
The '''helix piercing''' is any piercing through the rim of the [[index.php?title=Cartilage|cartilage]] (thus making it susceptible to complications such as [[index.php?title=Ear_Collapse|Ear Collapse]] if care is not taken to use proper tools and procedures; for example, [[index.php?title=Piercing_gun|Piercing guns]] have been shown to be capable of causing cartilage to shatter). The innermost part of the helix (closest to the head) is sometimes called an [[index.php?title=Ear_Head_Piercing|Ear Head Piercing]].
[[index.php?title=Paul_King|Paul King]] adds,
{{quote|&quot;In the West the helix piercing is considered only as adornment. However, helix piercings are found on every continent and in many traditional cultures have great importance.
The [[index.php?title=Dayak|Dayak]] of [[index.php?title=Borneo|Borneo]] (tribal names such as [[index.php?title=Iban|Iban]] and [[index.php?title=Punan&action=edit&redlink=1|Punan]]) would wear bear canines in their upper ear cartilage, often connected with intricate bead work. This was considered a sign of a brave headhunter. In the Lmasala clan of the [[index.php?title=Samburu&action=edit&redlink=1|Samburu]] (Kenya), the mothers of warriors wear their sons' circumcision earrings in their cartilage during their warriorhood.&quot;}}

== <span id="Alternative_Names" class="mw-headline"> Alternative Names </span> ==
; Ear Head Piercing
: a [[index.php?title=Local_Term|local term]] for '''helix piercings''' along the inner rim of the ear (ie. closest to the head). The earliest documented one in modern piercing was done by [[index.php?title=Crystal_Cross&action=edit&redlink=1|Crystal Cross]] on [[index.php?title=Elayne_Angel|Elayne Angel]].
